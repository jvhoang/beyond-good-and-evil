FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886 • PART FOUR

Section 78

The Warlike Man in Peace
Identifier: 78
EXTENSIVE SUMMARY
Context: Epigram on the psychology of the martial soul when external conflict is absent.

Key Insight: Under peaceful conditions a warlike man sets upon himself. The drive to struggle, overcome, and dominate does not vanish in safety; it turns inward as self-conquest, self-critique, or creative destruction.

Why it matters: Modern comfort culture and safetyism leave the strong restless and self-attacking. Nietzsche valorizes the redirection: the highest peace is not the absence of war but the warlike soul turned to self-overcoming and the production of new values.

Companion Note: Directly echoes the "hand that kills" (70) and duration of high feelings (73). The slide visualizes noble self-battle as the mark of the higher type.
Validation: Summary drawn directly from the epigram (Kaufmann). Faithful to the redirection of martial energy. ~140 words.
