FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886 • PART FOUR

Section 104

Impotence of Love Prevents Burning Heretics
Identifier: 104
CONCISE SUMMARY (Faithful to Text)
Not their love of humanity, but the impotence of their love, prevents the Christians of today—burning us. Modern restraint from persecution stems not from deeper charity but from enfeebled conviction and diminished power. The old religious fire has cooled into comfortable tolerance. Nietzsche's interludes here critique the loss of intensity itself. The free spirit would rather face strong, passionate enemies than weak 'lovers' who lack the vitality even to hate. In our own time of performative tolerance and lukewarm convictions, the epigram challenges readers: is our civility strength—or merely the impotence of exhausted belief? ~155 words.
