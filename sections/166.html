FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886 • PART FOUR

Section 166

The Mouth Lies, the Grimace Tells
Identifier: 166
Simple modern summary

You can lie with words, but your face and body often betray the truth anyway. Micro-expressions, tone, and awkward pauses leak what speech tries to hide. People read vibes even when they cannot prove the lie.

EXTENSIVE SUMMARY
Context

This section looks at why humans chase certainty and how our deepest drives shape what we call 'truth'.

Main argument
  • This section explores how our thinking is shaped by hidden drives.
  • We often value certainty and comfort more than uncomfortable truth.
  • In simple modern terms, this shows that people (and whole systems of thought) often disguise personal biases, fears, or power interests as pure objective truth — so we should stay alert to the real motives behind big ideas, including our own.
Key insight

Deception through words is common and relatively easy. full bodily deception is far more difficult. The face and gesture remain the most reliable, if subtle, windows onto inner states.

Modern companion insight

Today, in a time of curated language — press releases, social media posts, carefully scripted interviews — the body still leaks. Micro-expressions, tone shifts, and involuntary gestures continue to tell what polished speech tries to hide. The free spirit cultivates the habit of attending to the grimace as much as the sentence.

Key Context: Epigram on expression, deception, and the reliability of involuntary signs. Part Four psychological observations.
