FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886 • PART FOUR

Section 159

Repaying the Wrong Person
Identifier: 159
Simple modern summary

Nietzsche asks why we feel we must repay good and evil to the exact person who gave it to us. Grudges and loyalty often get redirected onto whoever is nearby or convenient. A lot of conflict is really about unpaid emotional debts aimed at the wrong target.

EXTENSIVE SUMMARY
Context

Basically: nietzsche notes the strong psychological imperative to balance accounts. to return good for good and ill for ill. while questioning the assumption that repayment must be directed at the original source.

Key Kaufmann quotes
  • Section 159 states: “One MUST repay good and ill; but why just to the person who did us good or ill?”
Main argument
  • Basically: the epigram liberates the will from the narrow logic of tit-for-tat while acknowledging its force.It opens possibilities for generosity that is not owed and for hostility that is not strictly deserved, bot...The free spirit can choose the object of repayment rather than being mechanically bound to the original actor.
Key insight

The imperative to repay is real, but the identity of the recipient is not fixed by the original transaction.

BGE connection

This belongs to the interludes’ exploration of morality as economy — revenge, gratitude, justice, and the will to power expressed through giving and taking. It connects to earlier remarks on pity, love as barbarity, and the criminal’s relation to the deed. Part Four repeatedly shows that moral phenomena are more fluid and strategic than they appear.

Modern companion insight

In 2026, call-out culture, restorative justice rituals, and social media gratitude performances insist on exact, public, targeted repayment — whether of harm or benefit. The logic is often petty and exhausting. The free spirit feels the drive to balance accounts but reserves the right to choose the time, form, and recipient. One may repay a slight by excelling elsewhere. one may return a kindness by passing it to a stranger or by creating something that no single benefactor could have foreseen. The accounts are settled, but not necessarily with the original party.

Key Context: Observation on the flexibility of repayment in the moral economy. Part Four psychology of reciprocity.
