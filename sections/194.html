FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886 • PART FIVE

Section 194

What Is Good? Different Answers
Identifier: 194
Simple modern summary

Different types of people answer what is good in totally different ways. Nobles prize power and self-affirmation; herd types prize safety and usefulness. There is no single good for everyone.

EXTENSIVE SUMMARY (Faithful to Text)
Context

Basically: different types answer the question differently according to their rank and needs.. The noble answer emphasizes power, splendor, self-affirmation.. The herd answer emphasizes utility, safety, absence of fear.

Main argument
  • Basically: nietzsche shows that even when the content of “good” overlaps, the signs by which one recognizes it differ ...
  • The higher type sees good in that which elevates and distinguishes the lower sees it in that which equalize...
Key insight

The difference between moralities is not only what they call good but what they take as sufficient evidence that good is present.

BGE connection

Prepares the master/slave distinction and the analysis of herd morality. Rank is visible in the criteria of valuation.

Modern companion insight

In 2026 politics and culture, “the good” is claimed by all sides, but the evidence each side accepts (equality metrics vs excellence metrics, safety vs risk, inclusion vs distinction) reveals the underlying type. The free spirit examines the criteria, not only the slogans.

Validation: Faithful to the section on differing signs of the good. ~280 words.
