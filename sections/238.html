FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886 • PART SEVEN

Section 238

The Fundamental Problem of "Man and Woman"
Identifier: 238
Simple modern summary

The relationship between men and women is one of life's deepest problems—not something you can fix with ideology or feelings alone. Shallow thinking flattens it into sameness; deeper thinking sees necessary tension built into life itself. Real depth means facing this tension without resentment or illusion.

EXTENSIVE SUMMARY (Faithful to Text)
Key Kaufmann quotes
  • “To be mistaken in the fundamental problem of ‘man and woman,’ to deny here the profoundest antagonism and the necessity for an eternally hostile tension, to dream here perhaps of equal rights, equal training, equal claims and obligations: that is a TYPICAL sign of shallow-mindedness; and a thinker who has proved himself shallow at this dangerous spot—shallow in instinct!—may generally be regarded as suspicious, nay more, as betrayed, as discovered...”
Context

Nietzsche declares the denial of deep, permanent sexual antagonism and the dream of full equalization a hallmark of shallowness. The thinker who stumbles here reveals an instinctual deficit. he will prove inadequate to the great questions of life. Depth requires acknowledging an “eternally hostile tension” and taking one’s stand upon “the immense rationality of Asia,” the stricter Greek development toward Oriental views of woman as the historical norm for strong cultures.

Main points
  • Equal rights rhetoric at this fundamental level is not progress but symptom. The profound man sees service and mission as woman’s predestined sphere and refuses the modern leveling that obscures rank and nature. To mistake this is not merely an intellectual error.
  • it's a diagnostic of character and capacity for truth.
Key insight

The problem of the sexes is not negotiable by ideology or sentiment. It's one of the deepest tests of instinctual honesty and philosophical depth. Shallow minds flatten it into equality. deeper ones recognize necessary tension and hierarchy rooted in life itself.

BGE connection

This directly extends §237’s epigrams and leads into §239 on the democratic “respect” that erodes woman’s modesty and taste. Together they form Nietzsche’s most concentrated critique in “Our Virtues” of modern sexual ideology. The section reinforces the book’s larger themes: gradations of rank, suspicion of herd moralities dressed as progress, and the demand that the free spirit descend to bodily and psychological realities rather than moral abstractions.

Modern companion insight

2026 sees intensified campaigns to treat sex as wholly socially constructed or infinitely malleable. Policy, education, and culture often punish acknowledgment of average differences in strength, psychology, or life strategy. The free spirit tests every claim against observable antagonism and complementarity. Depth today still consists in refusing the shallow dream at this “dangerous spot.” One doesn't need to endorse every historical stricture to recognize that denying the problem remains a marker of intellectual and instinctual superficiality. The future belongs to those who can think the tension without resentment or illusion.
