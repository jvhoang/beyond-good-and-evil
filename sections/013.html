FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886

Section 13

Identifier: 13
EXTENSIVE SUMMARY
Psychologists wrong to put self-preservation as cardinal instinct. A living thing seeks above all to DISCHARGE its strength—life itself is WILL TO POWER; self-preservation is only indirect result. Beware superfluous teleological principles. This is a landmark introduction of the will to power concept in BGE. Key quote as extracted. Implications: Morality and psychology must be rethought in terms of power discharge rather than preservation or pleasure. Central to Nietzsche's philosophy of life-affirmation and critique of all life-denying morals. Fits perfectly in Part One as the positive physiological/ontological ground for the critiques.
Key Context: Part of Nietzsche's systematic critique in BGE Preface and Part One. Emphasizes perspectivism, will to power, and revaluation of values.
