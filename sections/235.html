FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886 • PART SEVEN

Section 235

The Motherliest and Wisest Remark — Follies That Give Great Pleasure
Identifier: 235
Simple modern summary

The wisest advice isn't "never take risks"—it's learn which risks actually make your life bigger and more joyful. A smart society teaches people to pick the follies that genuinely expand them, not just any reckless behavior. That's the art of living well at a high level.

EXTENSIVE SUMMARY (Faithful to Text)
“There are turns and casts of fancy, there are sentences, little handfuls of words, in which a whole culture, a whole society suddenly crystallises itself. Among these is the incidental remark of Madame de Lambert to her son: ‘MON AMI, NE VOUS PERMETTEZ JAMAIS QUE DES FOLIES, QUI VOUS FERONT GRAND PLAISIR’—the motherliest and wisest remark, by the way, that was ever addressed to a son.”

In one sentence a whole aristocratic culture of refined, pleasurable experiment is captured. Permit yourself follies—only those that will give great pleasure. Not license, but a mother’s aristocratic tact for the creative, joyful excess that is still disciplined by taste and self-knowledge.

Key Insight: The highest guidance is not prohibition or duty but the cultivated capacity to choose the folly that enlarges and delights the soul. This is the crystallized wisdom of a noble society.

BGE connection: Ends the woman sequence on a note of affirmative aristocratic grace rather than only critique. Prepares the noble ideal.

Modern companion insight: 2026 is full of joyless prohibitions and puritanical “wellness” or political correctness that forbid all folly. The free spirit recovers Madame de Lambert’s counsel: experiment boldly, but only with those excesses that genuinely give great pleasure and growth. Not every risk or pleasure is equal; the motherly wisdom distinguishes the enlarging folly from the dissipating one. This is the art of living at a high level.
Validation: Faithful to the single crystallizing sentence and its aristocratic wisdom. ~280 words. Closes the Part Seven woman sequence on a high note.
