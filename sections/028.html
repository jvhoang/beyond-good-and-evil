FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886

Section 28

Identifier: 28 • Part Two: The Free Spirit
EXTENSIVE SUMMARY
The free spirit’s relation to language and tempo: every nation has its own tempo of language and therefore of thought. The French, with their light, rapid, elegant style, have a different intellectual rhythm from the Germans. The free spirit must find or create his own tempo, often slower, more deliberate, or more playful than the national average. Style is the man; tempo is the soul.

This section continues the exploration of the free spirit’s distinctiveness in expression and pace. It underscores that genuine thought has its own music and that national or conventional styles can constrain or distort it.

Key Takeaway: Thought and language have an intimate, national, and personal tempo. The free spirit cultivates a style and pace suited to his own multiplicity and depth rather than conforming to the prevailing national or popular rhythm.

Connections to the broader BGE project: Extends the critique of herd thinking and national prejudices into the realm of style and communication. Prepares the reader for the free spirit’s experimental use of language and masks in later sections. Style as the man echoes the Preface’s view of philosophy as personal confession.

Reading Tip (Kaufmann translation): Nietzsche himself writes in a highly varied tempo within BGE—aphoristic, rapid, then lingering. The section rewards attention to the actual rhythms of the prose as one reads.

Why it matters today: In a global, English-dominated, social-media-accelerated culture of short attention and rapid-fire communication, Nietzsche’s insistence on personal and cultural tempo is liberating. Writers, thinkers, and even coders or designers can reclaim slower, more deliberate, or more idiosyncratic rhythms without apology. It also invites reflection on how platform algorithms enforce a single, frantic tempo on thought and discourse.
Companion Polish: The free spirit’s style is not affectation but fidelity to his own inner music. Conforming to the wrong tempo distorts both thought and life.
