FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886

Section 31

Identifier: 31 • Part Two: The Free Spirit
EXTENSIVE SUMMARY
Section 31 describes the transition from youth's unconditional veneration and contempt (the worst taste) to the art of nuances. Youth forges men and things to vent its wrath or reverence; later it turns against itself in suspicion and self-torture. All this, ten years later, is still recognized as youth.

In context, this humanizes the free spirit's development: the path to subtlety and the dangerous "Perhaps" requires passing through and overcoming youthful dogmatism and self-laceration.

Key quotes and implications: the free spirit learns the art of nuances; power in risking the artificial; advances critique by showing even the critic's path is a youthful phase to be outgrown.
Key Context: Psychological development of the free spirit.
