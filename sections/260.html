FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886 • PART NINE

Section 260

Master Morality and Slave Morality
Identifier: 260
Simple modern summary

Every higher culture mixes two moralities: noble souls who create values from abundance, and reactive slave morality that levels through resentment and utility. Today's "progressive" values often show slave-moral traits (safety, pity, suspicion of strength). Recognizing both types in yourself and culture is step one toward creating something new.

EXTENSIVE SUMMARY (Faithful to Text)
Context

Every higher culture mixes two moralities. Noble souls create values from abundance and strength. Reactive slave morality levels through resentment and utility. "Progressive" values today often show slave-moral traits like safety, pity, and suspicion of strength.

Main points
  • Every higher culture mixes noble morality (creating values from abundance) and slave morality (leveling through resentment and utility).
  • Today progressive values often show slave-moral traits: emphasis on safety, pity, and suspicion of strength.
  • Master-moral residues appear in competitive excellence, self-reverence, and the will to create new standards.
  • Recognizing both types in yourself and culture is the first step toward creating something new.
the will to create new standards.
Key Insights

All higher cultures contain both moralities in tension or mixture. The noble soul creates values from its own plenitude and affirms distance and rank. morality of the weak reacts against the noble, inverts values through utility and resentment, and levels. The philosopher of the future must understand this genealogy to create beyond both in a higher synthesis.

Modern Companion Insight

Contemporary "progressive" values often display classic slave-moral traits (emphasis on safety, utility, universal pity, suspicion of strength and excellence). Master-moral residues appear in competitive excellence, self-reverence, and the will to create new standards. Recognizing the two types within ourselves and culture is essential for any genuine rethinking of values.
