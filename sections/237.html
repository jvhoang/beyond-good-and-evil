FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886 • PART SEVEN

Section 237

Seven Apophthegms for Women
Identifier: 237
EXTENSIVE SUMMARY (Faithful to Text)
“How the longest ennui flees, / When a man comes to our knees! / Age, alas! and science staid, / Furnish even weak virtue aid. / Sombre garb and silence meet: / Dress for every dame—discreet. / Whom I thank when in my bliss? / God!—and my good tailoress! / Young, a flower-decked cavern home; / Old, a dragon thence doth roam. / Noble title, leg that’s fine, / Man as well: Oh, were HE mine! / Speech in brief and sense in mass— / Slippery for the jenny-ass!”

Nietzsche presents a set of seven witty, ironic apophthegms “for women.” These concise rhymes offer practical counsel on marriage, aging, dress, gratitude, youth and age, social ambition, and speech. They portray feminine life as strategic navigation of ennui, appearance, and social position, laced with self-aware humor and a certain realism about power, desire, and limitation.

The verses do not flatter. They treat woman as a player in a game of attraction and status, where virtue is aided by age and learning, where dress and silence are armor, where a good tailor and a good husband are parallel blessings, where youth is a “flower-decked cavern” and age a dragon, and where brevity of speech paired with depth of meaning trips up the foolish. The final line mocks those who cannot handle concise wisdom.

Key Insight: These are not mere jokes; they distill an unsentimental psychology of the sexes as Nietzsche saw it. Woman’s “virtues” in this frame are adaptive tactics within an eternal tension of attraction, rank, and survival rather than abstract moral ideals imposed from outside.

BGE connection: In Part Seven, “Our Virtues,” Nietzsche interrogates what counts as virtue for modern Europeans, including the sexes. Preceding sections on multiple moralities and the problem of pity lead here to direct, aphoristic commentary on sexual difference. Section 237 and the following 238–239 form a concentrated examination of the “fundamental problem of ‘man and woman.’” They reject shallow egalitarian flattening and insist on recognizing deep, perhaps ineradicable antagonisms and complementary instincts.

Modern companion insight: In 2026, with gender ideology, social media performance of femininity, and policy debates over differences, these apophthegms read as defiant realism. The free spirit refuses both sentimental idealization and ideological denial of sexed psychology. Strategic self-presentation, the role of appearance and speech, the reality of age and attraction, and the comedy of mismatched expectations remain observable facts. One studies the rhymes not to endorse every line but to recover clarity about the game before pretending the rules have been abolished. The jenny-ass still stumbles over sense in mass.
