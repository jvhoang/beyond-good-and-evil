FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886 • PART FOUR

Section 179

Consequences Seize Us Indifferent to Reform
Identifier: 179
Simple modern summary

Life grabs you by the collar for what you did, even if you have changed since then. Reforming your character does not erase old actions or their fallout. The past keeps collecting interest.

EXTENSIVE SUMMARY (Faithful to Text)
Context

Reform or moral improvement after the fact does not annul the chain of effects set in motion. Consequences are not moral agents; they are causal. They do not pause to ask whether we have since become better people.

Key Kaufmann quotes
  • “The consequences of our actions seize us by the forelock, very indifferent to the fact that we have meanwhile &#x27;reformed.&#x27;”
Main argument

This is both a warning against sentimental views of redemption and a liberation: one need not be paralyzed by past deeds once their consequences are faced. The past is not erased by good intentions

it is outrun or incorporated.

Key insight

Moral change is real for the agent but irrelevant to the objective train of effects. Responsibility is not dissolved by later virtue.

BGE connection

Supports the naturalistic view of action and its results over moralistic narratives of conversion and absolution.

Modern companion insight

“I’ve changed” or “I’ve grown” is often offered as sufficient reply to past harm. The free spirit acknowledges that consequences roll on independently and that genuine responsibility includes bearing what one has set in motion without the consolation of retroactive innocence.
