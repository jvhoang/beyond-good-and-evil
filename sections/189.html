FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886 • PART FIVE

Section 189

Fasts, Industriousness, and Sublimation
Identifier: 189
Simple modern summary

Boring rest days and forced breaks can make people crave work again, which cultures use to channel energy productively. Discipline works by redirecting drives, not deleting them. Even idleness can be engineered for control.

EXTENSIVE SUMMARY (Faithful to Text)
Context

Nietzsche observes that powerful impulses require periodic restraint. The English Sunday is a brilliant cultural invention that turns idleness into a fast, sharpening appetite for labor. Moral fanaticisms and religious disciplines function similarly: they force an impulse to go hungry, to purify and intensify itself. The result can be refinement and transfiguration rather than mere suppression.

Key Kaufmann quotes
  • “The industrious races complain a great deal about having to tolerate idleness: it was a masterpiece of the English instinct to make Sunday so holy and so tedious, a form of cleverly invented and shrewdly introduced fasting, that the Englishman... became eager again for weekdays and workdays... Seen from a higher viewpoint, the periods when entire races and ages get afflicted with some moral fanaticism or other look like such imposed times of compulsion and fasting, during which an impulse learns to cower down and abase itself, but also to cleanse itself and become sharper... And with this is also given a hint for an explanation of that paradox why it was precisely in Europe&#x27;s Christian period... that the sex drive sublimated itself into love (amour-passion).”
Main argument

The sublimation of sexuality into passionate love is offered as one historical example of such disciplined intensification under Christian pressure.

Key insight

Periods of imposed restraint and fasting are not merely negative. they are often the means by which strong drives become sharper, cleaner, and capable of higher forms.

BGE connection

Continues the theme that constraint and tyranny (not laisser-aller) produce culture and spirituality. Links directly to 188’s praise of long obedience.

Modern companion insight

2026’s always-on digital culture and instant gratification erode the capacity for voluntary or cultural fasting. The loss of meaningful Sabbaths, disciplines, or seasons of restraint may be costing us depth in work, love, and art. Self-overcoming requires deliberate insertion of limits and fasts so that drives don't dissipate in easy satisfaction but reach sharper, higher expressions.
