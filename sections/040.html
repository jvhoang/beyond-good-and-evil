FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886

Section 40

Identifier: 40 • Part Two: The Free Spirit
Simple modern summary

Deep thinkers need masks because shallow people will misread everything they do. Concealment is not fake—it protects what is fragile and rare inside. In a world that rewards oversharing, real depth often requires privacy and strategic distance.

EXTENSIVE SUMMARY
Context

Basically: section 40 opens with one of Nietzsche's most memorable and profound images.. The profound spirit requires a mask.. Around every profound spirit a mask continually grows because of the constantly false, shallow interpretati...

Main points
  • Basically: shame is inventive.
  • The free spirit learns concealment.
  • Masks are not mere deception but a necessary defense and even a gracious cunning.
  • The profound individual may appear rude or ordinary to protect what is delicate and vulnerable within.
  • Such a person's mortal dangers and regained sureness remain hidden even from intimates.
  • A mask of him roams among friends whether he wills it or not.
Key Kaufmann quotes
Key Kaufmann quotes
  • “Every profound spirit needs a mask: even more, around every profound spirit a mask is growing continually, owing to the constantly false, namely shallow, interpretation of every word, every step, every sign of life he gives.”
Modern companion insight

In today's always-on social media culture that rewards oversharing, performative authenticity, and instant personal branding, Nietzsche reminds us that real depth often requires masks, privacy, strategic distance, and selective revelation. The strongest souls cultivate the art of concealment to preserve what is rare and precious from the shallow gaze of the herd. This is not duplicity — it's self-preservation and courtesy of the spirit.

Key Context: Masks as essential to the free spirit (echoing Sec 25); deepens the psychology of independence and prepares "riddles" of Sec 42.
