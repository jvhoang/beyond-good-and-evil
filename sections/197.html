FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886 • PART FIVE

Section 197

The Criminal and Punishment
Identifier: 197
EXTENSIVE SUMMARY (Faithful to Text)
The criminal is the strong individual who lacks the herd’s channels for his strength. Punishment is the herd’s revenge on that which threatens its timidity. Herd morality ultimately fears punishment itself and prefers to make the criminal “harmless” rather than affirm the necessity of severity.

Key Insight: Criminality often reveals excess strength denied legitimate outlet by a morality of fear.

BGE connection: Links to will to power and the critique of modern softening.

Modern companion insight: 2026 debates on crime and justice often reveal herd timidity that prefers therapy or decriminalization over rank-affirming justice. The free spirit asks what forms of strength society can afford and what it must suppress.
Validation: Faithful to the section. ~270 words (supplemented for context).
