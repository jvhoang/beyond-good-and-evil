FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886 • PART THREE

Section 60

Love of Mankind for God's Sake — The Noblest and Remotest Sentiment
Identifier: 60
Simple modern summary

The highest love of humanity is not generic kindness—it is love for God's sake, which needs refinement and distance to avoid becoming crude. Without a higher frame, even noble feelings can turn foolish and brutal. The finest souls are the ones who almost see too much.

EXTENSIVE SUMMARY (Faithful to Text)
Key Quotes

Context

In discussing religious feeling, Nietzsche praises the idea of loving humanity not for its own sake but for God's sake. Direct love of people without a higher ideal behind it easily turns into something foolish or even brutal. The person who first understood that true love for mankind needs distance, refinement, and a higher purpose deserves our respect.

Main points
  • Loving people simply because they are people often leads to crude, foolish, or harmful actions without any guiding higher standard.
  • The noblest form of love for humanity has come from seeing people in relation to God or some higher ideal, which adds delicacy and proper distance.
  • Without that higher frame or redeeming intention, even good intentions can make things worse rather than better.
  • The first thinkers who grasped this delicate truth about love should be honored, even if they struggled to put it into words.
