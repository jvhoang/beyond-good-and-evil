FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886 • PART EIGHT

Section 256

Europe Wants to Be One: The Good Europeans
Identifier: 256
EXTENSIVE SUMMARY (Faithful to Text)
Section 256 diagnoses the nationality-craze as an interlude: “Owing to the morbid estrangement which the nationality-craze has induced and still induces among the nations of Europe... the most unmistakable signs that EUROPE WISHES TO BE ONE, are now overlooked... With all the more profound and large-minded men of this century, the real general tendency of the mysterious labour of their souls was to prepare the way for that new SYNTHESIS... Napoleon, Goethe, Beethoven, Stendhal, Heinrich Heine, Schopenhauer... Richard Wagner... All of them... the first artists of universal literary culture... fanatics for EXPRESSION 'at any cost'...”

They rested from themselves only when they became patriots. The task is the European of the future.

Key Insight: The deepest spirits of the century, despite momentary fatherland fits, worked toward European synthesis. Nationalism is a passing sickness hiding the will to oneness.

BGE connection: Climax of Part Eight. After national portraits and warnings against English and German limitations, Nietzsche names the great task: the good European who overcomes peoples and fatherlands.

Modern companion insight: Resurgent nationalisms, identity politics, and protectionism repeat the interlude. The free spirit sees through the noise to the underlying pressure toward larger synthesis—economic, cultural, perhaps political—and prepares the new ruling type capable of commanding a united Europe or its global equivalent, without the cheap patriotism of the surface.
Validation: Direct Kaufmann of Section 256. Faithful to the overlooked signs of European unity and the list of great seekers preparing the synthesis. ~300 words.
