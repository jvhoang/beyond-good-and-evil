FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886 • PART FOUR

Section 102

Disenchanted by Reciprocal Love
Identifier: 102
Simple modern summary

Mutual love should actually burst your romantic bubble. If she loves you too, maybe her standards are not as high as you thought—or yours are not either. Reciprocity exposes ordinariness under the fantasy.

EXTENSIVE SUMMARY
Discovering reciprocal love should really disenchant the lover with regard to the beloved. "What! She is modest enough to love even you? Or stupid enough? Or—or—-" Mutual love, far from crowning the ideal, exposes the ordinariness of both parties and the low standards required for such reciprocity. The romantic enchantment collapses under the weight of this recognition. Nietzsche offers a sharp, unsentimental view of love as often little more than mutual ego-projection. The free spirit registers the disenchantment cleanly, without bitterness or despair. In an age of algorithmic matching and endless romantic myth-making, the epigram remains a bracing reminder: genuine eros may demand we see the beloved (and ourselves) without the softening filter of idealization. ~155 words.
