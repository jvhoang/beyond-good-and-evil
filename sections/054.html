FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886

Section 54

Identifier: 54 • Part Two: The Free Spirit
EXTENSIVE SUMMARY
Section 54 argues that modern philosophy since Descartes has, under the guise of critiquing subject-predicate logic, attacked the Christian conception of the soul. Epistemological skepticism is secretly or openly anti-Christian, though not necessarily anti-religious. The attempt to derive the 'I' from thinking itself (rather than the reverse) echoes the Vedanta. Kant wished to prove that neither subject nor object could be proved from the subject.

Key Insight: The 'soul' and the substantial 'I' are grammatical prejudices, not metaphysical certainties.

Modern companion insight: Cognitive science and phenomenology continue this deconstruction. The free spirit welcomes the dissolution of the naive ego without falling into nihilism, seeing it as liberation for new valuations.
Key Context: Epigram from Part Two. Core to free spirit's honesty and critique of weakness.
