FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886

Section 34

Identifier: 34 • Part Two: The Free Spirit
Simple modern summary

The world we live in is probably wrong in basic ways—and that might be the most solid thing we know. Thinking truth always beats appearance is just a moral habit, not a fact. Philosophers should stop trusting grammar and simple either-or logic.

EXTENSIVE SUMMARY
Context

We often misunderstand the motives of others because we project our own drives onto them.

The psychologist must be very careful not to fool himself about why people act.

Main points
  • What we take for another person's action is often our own interpretation.
  • We are poor observers of ourselves and others.
  • Motives are complex and often hidden even from the actor.
  • The will to power can look like many other things from the outside.
