FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886 • PART FOUR

Section 105

Pia Fraus More Repugnant to Free Spirit
Identifier: 105
CONCISE SUMMARY (Faithful to Text)
The pia fraus is still more repugnant to the taste (the "piety") of the free spirit (the "pious man of knowledge") than the impia fraus. Hence the profound lack of judgment, in comparison with the Church, characteristic of the type "free spirit"—as ITS non-freedom. The free spirit—pious in the pursuit of knowledge—finds the holy lie (pia fraus) more offensive than outright impious deceit. This very rigidity of judgment reveals the type's own unfreedom. In the interludes Nietzsche turns the lens inward: even the 'free spirit' carries its characteristic blindness and lack of suppleness. The insight is a rare moment of self-critique within BGE, reminding the reader that intellectual honesty has its own prejudices and that true freedom may require overcoming even the piety of knowledge. ~160 words.
