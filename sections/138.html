FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886 • PART FOUR

Section 138

We Invent in Waking and Dreaming
Identifier: 138
Simple modern summary

Awake or dreaming, we invent the people we interact with and forget we did. You partly create who you think you are meeting. Memory erases the fabrication so illusions feel real.

EXTENSIVE SUMMARY
Context

Section 138 states.

Key insight

Whether asleep or awake, we fabricate the interlocutor or companion we need at the moment. The person we “meet” is largely our own construction, tailored to desire, fear, or narrative. Once the encounter ends, the fabrication is quickly forgotten so that we can maintain the useful illusion of having engaged with a real, stable other. Social reality is heavily invented by each participant. memory conveniently erases the creative labor so the illusion can persist.

BGE connection

This epigram extends Nietzsche’s perspectivism and psychology of interpretation into everyday intercourse. It echoes the larger claim that we deal not with “things in themselves” but with our interpretations and needs. The free spirit becomes conscious of this constant invention rather than remaining its unwitting dupe.

Modern companion

In 2026 the phenomenon is intensified by digital mediation: we invent elaborate personas for colleagues on Slack, dates on apps, influencers on feeds, and even family in group chats — then rapidly forget how much we supplied. Deepfakes and AI companions make the invention literal. The free spirit practices deliberate awareness of the projections he brings to every interaction and cultivates the rare art of meeting the actual, resistant other rather than his convenient fabrication. Forgetting the invention is the normal mechanism. remembering it's the beginning of honesty.

Key Context: Epigram on the invented nature of social reality and convenient forgetting. Part Four perspectival psychology.
