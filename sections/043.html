FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886

Section 43

Identifier: 43 • Part Two: The Free Spirit
Simple modern summary

Future philosophers will not chase agreement with the crowd or shared feel-good truths. What is common is usually shallow, and great things belong to great minds. Nuance, depth, and rarity should stay rare—not flattened for everyone.

EXTENSIVE SUMMARY
Key Kaufmann quotes
  • “Are these coming philosophers new friends of 'truth'? ... They will certainly not be dogmatists.”
  • “My judgment is my judgment”
Context

Basically: one must shed the bad taste of wanting to agree with many. The term contradicts itself.

Key Kaufmann quotes
Key Kaufmann quotes
  • “In the end it must be as it is and always has been: great things remain for the great, abysses for the profound, nuances and shudders for the refined, and, in brief, all that is rare for the rare.”
Modern companion insight

Against today's flattening "diversity," "inclusion," and "equity" rhetoric, safe spaces, and the demand for universal agreement on every moral, scientific, or political question, Nietzsche champions intellectual honesty about differences in capacity, taste, rank, and insight. The rare and profound deserve their own domains. forcing everything into the "common" or "accessible to all" diminishes what is highest and most enhancing for humanity.

Key Context: Anti-egalitarian, anti-dogmatic stance of the future philosophers; pathos of distance and rank of souls.
