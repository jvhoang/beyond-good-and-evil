FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886 • PART NINE

Section 279

Bad! He Goes Back Like One About to Make a Great Spring
Identifier: 279
Simple modern summary

When someone pulls back, it might look like failure—but they're often winding up for a bigger leap. Strategic withdrawal gets misread by people who can't see the spring coming. Retreat isn't always defeat.

EXTENSIVE SUMMARY (Faithful to Text)
"Bad! Bad! What? Does he not--go back?" Yes! But you misunderstand him when you complain about it. He goes back like every one who is about to make a great spring.

Key Insight: Apparent retreat or regression is often preparation for a greater leap forward.

Modern companion insight: Strategic withdrawal is misread as failure by those who do not see the coming spring.
Validation: Faithful. ~120 words.
