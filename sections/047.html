FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886 • PART THREE

Section 47

Religious Neurosis, Solitude, Fasting, and the Saint
Identifier: 47
Simple modern summary

The saint looks miraculous, but Nietzsche treats that as religious neurosis—extreme swings between pleasure and guilt, often tied to solitude, fasting, and celibacy. Philosophers and artists romanticize this type, yet it is more about will and psychology than holiness. Do not confuse self-torture with spiritual greatness.
