Section 113: Embarrassment Before the Other

Part Four: Epigrams and Interludes • Beyond Good and Evil

Simple modern summary

To win someone over, show a little embarrassment—not smooth dominance. Calculated confidence repels; awkward humanity disarms. Charm often needs vulnerability, not swagger.

EXTENSIVE SUMMARY

"You want to prepossess him in your favour? Then you must be embarrassed before him."

To win favor or influence, one must display a certain embarrassment or vulnerability before the other. Smooth confidence or superiority does not charm; a touch of awkwardness or self-consciousness signals respect, humanity, and openness that disarms.

The psychological reversal: calculated poise may repel, while genuine (or artful) embarrassment attracts and prepossesses. This touches BGE themes of masks, power, and the subtle arts of influence among higher types.

Modern companion insight: In networking, dating, leadership, or persuasion, over-polished self-presentation can signal threat or inauthenticity. A measured vulnerability or embarrassment humanizes and builds rapport more effectively than flawless performance. ~160 words.
