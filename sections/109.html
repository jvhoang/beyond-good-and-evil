Section 109: The Criminal and His Deed

Part Four: Epigrams and Interludes • Beyond Good and Evil

Simple modern summary

Criminals often are not big enough for their own crimes—they downplay or trash what they did. Most people shrink their actions to fit a smaller self-image. Own your deeds or excuses will own you.

EXTENSIVE SUMMARY
Key Quotes
  • The criminal is often enough not equal to his deed: he extenuates and maligns it.
Context

Often the person who commits a crime cannot live up to the size of what they did. They make excuses or badmouth their own action to make it seem smaller.

Main points
  • Criminals often cannot match the scale of their own crime in their self image.
  • They shrink or slander the deed after the fact to feel better about it.
  • Most people make their actions smaller so they fit a modest view of themselves.
  • Really owning what you have done requires facing its full weight.
Key Insights

People shrink big actions, good or bad, to protect how they see themselves.

Connection to the book

Part of the interludes on self-deception, crime, and how we interpret deeds through our drives.

Modern Companion Insight

In news and social media, terrible events get explained away or sensationalized instead of faced for their full human reality.
