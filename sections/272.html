FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886 • PART NINE

Section 272

The Problem of Those Who Wait
Identifier: 272
EXTENSIVE SUMMARY (Faithful to Text)
Happy chances and incalculable elements are necessary for a higher man in whom the solution of a problem is dormant to "break forth" at the right moment. On average it does not happen. There are waiting ones in every corner who hardly know they wait, or wait in vain. The call sometimes comes too late; limbs are benumbed, spirits heavy. "It is too late."

In the domain of genius, the "Raphael without hands" may be the rule rather than the exception. Genius is not so rare; the five hundred hands required to tyrannize over the right time are.

Key Insight: Higher types often remain unrealized because the moment never aligns; many are the geniuses who never acted because opportunity came too late.

Modern companion insight: In fast, noisy cultures, the patient waiting of the higher type is undervalued. The free spirit prepares for the rare alignment of capacity and chance.
Validation: Faithful to the aphorism on waiting and the rarity of timely action. ~220 words.
