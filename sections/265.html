FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886 • PART NINE

Section 265

Egoism as the Essence of the Noble Soul
Identifier: 265
Simple modern summary

Noble egoism isn't petty selfishness—it's natural self-respect and knowing your place in the order of things. Modern culture treats any self-assertion as vice, but the higher type knows they stand above without apologizing. Exceptional people standing out is just reality.

EXTENSIVE SUMMARY (Faithful to Text)
Key Quotes
  • “At the risk of displeasing innocent ears, I submit that egoism belongs to the essence of a noble soul, I mean the unalterable belief that to a being such as 'we,' other beings must naturally be in subjection, and have to sacrifice themselves.”
  • “It is justice itself.”
Context

Noble egoism is natural self-respect and knowing your place in the order. It is not petty selfishness. Exceptional people standing out is just reality.

Main points
  • Egoism belongs to the essence of a noble soul, the unaltered faith that to a being such as "we" other beings must be subordinate.
  • Noble egoism is self-respect, not petty selfishness.
  • Modern culture treats any self-assertion as vice, but the higher type stands above without apology.
  • Exceptional people standing out is simply reality.
Key Insights

Noble egoism is not petty selfishness but the natural self-reverence and order-of-rank awareness of the higher type. It's just that the exceptional stand above and that others may serve the higher.

Modern Companion Insight

Modern culture often equates any self-assertion or rank-awareness with vice. Nietzsche restores the legitimacy of the noble soul's natural perspective: one is on a height and knows it without apology.

Validation: Direct from text. Captures the unapologetic egoism, justice among unequals, peer intercourse, and the knowledge of height. ~280 words.
