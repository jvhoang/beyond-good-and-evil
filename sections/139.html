FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886 • PART FOUR

Section 139

Woman More Barbarous in Revenge and Love
Identifier: 139
EXTENSIVE SUMMARY
Section 139 states: “In revenge and in love woman is more barbarous than man.”

When passion—whether erotic attachment or the desire for retribution—is aroused, women are capable of greater extremity, cruelty, or abandon than men. The observation does not moralize; it registers a difference in intensity and threshold. Civilization and restraint may sit more lightly on the surface in these domains for women.

Key Insight: In the two great spheres of erotic love and vengeance, the female capacity for the unmediated, the ruthless, and the total often exceeds the male.

Connection to BGE themes: This belongs to Nietzsche’s series of gender observations in Part Four that challenge sentimental or egalitarian pieties. It contributes to his broader project of describing human types and drives without moral overlay. The free spirit notes such differences in intensity without using them to justify conventional moral hierarchies or modern ideological denials.

Modern companion insight: In 2026 social media feuds, relationship dissolutions, workplace vendettas, and cancel campaigns, the pattern of greater female intensity in love and revenge remains visible—sometimes celebrated as empowerment, sometimes pathologized. The free spirit registers the observation without either romanticizing or condemning. It refuses both the old chivalric lie that women are gentler by nature and the new lie that gender differences in passion are entirely social constructs. Clear sight includes acknowledging that in certain drives the sexes differ in their thresholds for barbarism.
Key Context: Epigram on gendered intensities in passion and retribution. Part Four unromantic observations on human drives.
