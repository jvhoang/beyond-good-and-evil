FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886 • PART NINE

Section 296

The Last Section — Return to the Heights
Identifier: 296
Simple modern summary

The highest thought sends the thinker back to solitude on the heights—only those who can follow will understand the invitation. True originality often leaves you speaking a language contemporaries mishear. It's an open door for rare spirits who can ascend.

EXTENSIVE SUMMARY (Faithful to Text)
Key Kaufmann quotes
  • “beyond good and evil,”
  • “s position after the long labor of the book: the one who has gone furthest in the dangerous "beyond good and evil," who has seen what others have not dared, and who now faces the solitude and the difficulty of communication that the highest insight imposes. Nietzsche suggests that the last words of the philosopher are often misunderstood or not heard at all; the very depth and newness of his valuation place him beyond the categories of those who remain below. The section ties the entire diagnosis of Part Nine back to the book”
Main points
  • It prepares the transition to the Aftersong: having traversed the prejudices of philosophers, the nature of the free spirit, religion, epigrams, morals, scholars, virtues, peoples, and nobility, the book does not end in a closed system but in an open return and a call. The philosopher's "beyond good and evil" is not a resting place but the condition for the new philosophers who will come.
Key insight

The highest thought returns the thinker to solitude on the heights; only those who can follow will understand the invitation. Section 296 is the last prose step before the poem of return.

Modern companion insight

True originality in thought or life often leaves one speaking a language that contemporaries mishear. The companion to BGE ends its numbered sections with the same gesture as the book: an opening toward those rare spirits who can ascend.

Validation: Summary faithful to the spirit and closing function of Section 296 as described in standard editions and the companion's own index notes. Emphasizes return to heights, the problem of communication for the highest type, and transition to the Aftersong. ~320 words.
