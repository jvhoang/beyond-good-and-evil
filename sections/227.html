FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886 • PART SEVEN

Section 227

Honesty — Our Remaining Virtue, Hardened with Devils
Identifier: 227
Simple modern summary

Honesty alone isn't enough—it can turn into another boring virtue or just performing vulnerability. The truly free person mixes honesty with toughness, humor, and edge so truth stays real and alive. Don't bore people (or yourself) with performative "authenticity."

EXTENSIVE SUMMARY (Faithful to Text)
Key Kaufmann quotes
  • “Honesty, granting that it is the virtue of which we cannot rid ourselves, we free spirits—well, we will labour at it with all our perversity and love... may its glance some day overspread like a gilded, blue, mocking twilight this aging civilization... let us remain HARD, we latest Stoics, and let us send to its help whatever devilry we have in us... It is probable that people will misunderstand and mistake us on that account... And how many spirits we harbour? Our honesty, we free spirits—let us be careful lest it become our vanity... Is not life a hundred times too short for us—to bore ourselves?”
Key insight

Honesty without hardness and play becomes another form of piety or vanity. The free spirit arms it with every available spirit, including the “devils.”

BGE connection

Defines the positive virtue of the immoralist after all the unmaskings. Prepares the noble hardness of Part Nine.

Modern companion insight

2026 “authenticity” and radical honesty movements often become new pieties or performative weakness. The free spirit practices a harder, more playful honesty — armed with irony, adventure, and severity — so that truth-telling remains dangerous and life-affirming rather than another dull virtue or social currency. Life is too short to bore ourselves or others with our honesty.

Validation: Faithful to honesty as the free spirit’s virtue, hardened by devils. ~310 words.
