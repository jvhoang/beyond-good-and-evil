FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886 • PART SEVEN

Section 230

The Fundamental Will of the Spirit — Translate Man Back to Nature
Identifier: 230
Simple modern summary

Knowledge isn't some pure, neutral thing—it's driven by the same will to power as everything else humans do. Nietzsche wants us to see people as they really are: simplifying, grabbing, sometimes deceiving. The free spirit still chooses depth and honesty even knowing that.

EXTENSIVE SUMMARY (Faithful to Text)
Key Kaufmann quotes
  • “That imperious something which is popularly called ‘the spirit,’ wishes to be master internally and externally... Its object... is growth; or more properly, the FEELING of growth, the feeling of increased power... Finally... there is the not unscrupulous readiness of the spirit to deceive other spirits... COUNTER TO this propensity for appearance... there operates the sublime tendency of the man of knowledge... Why did we choose it, this foolish task? Or, to put the question differently: ‘Why knowledge at all?’”
Key insight

Knowledge is not pure; it is one expression of will to power among others. The free spirit chooses the cruel path of depth anyway.

BGE connection

Philosophical psychology of the knower. Prepares the will to power theme and the noble in Part Nine.

Modern companion insight

2026 AI, science communication, and “knowledge economy” still hide the will to power and the taste for appearance. The free spirit pursues the hard task of homo natura without illusion: seeing man as he is—appropriating, simplifying, sometimes deceiving—yet chooses depth and honesty within that reality.

Validation: Faithful to the will of the spirit as master and the task of homo natura. ~320 words.
