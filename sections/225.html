FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886 • PART SEVEN

Section 225

The Discipline of Suffering — Pity Against Pity
Identifier: 225
Simple modern summary

Nietzsche flips the script on suffering—instead of trying to erase all pain, he says hard times are what actually push humanity to grow. The kind of sympathy that just cushions people can actually make them weaker. Real growth comes from facing tough stuff head-on, not from a pain-free life.

EXTENSIVE SUMMARY (Faithful to Text)
“You want, if possible—and there is no more insane ‘if possible’—TO ABOLISH SUFFERING; and we?—it really seems that WE would rather have it increased and made worse than it has ever been! ... The discipline of suffering, of GREAT suffering—know ye not that it is only THIS discipline that has produced all the elevations of humanity hitherto? ... In man CREATURE and CREATOR are united... And our sympathy—do ye not understand what our REVERSE sympathy applies to, when it resists your sympathy as the worst of all pampering and enervation?—So it is sympathy AGAINST sympathy!”

All hedonistic, utilitarian, eudaemonist systems are naive. The free spirit with creative conscience looks down on the obsession with abolishing suffering. Great suffering is the discipline that forges higher humanity; creature and creator require it. Our pity resists the pity that enervates.

Key Insight: Suffering is not an argument against life but the necessary forge of elevation. The highest sympathy may demand more suffering, not less, for the sake of rank and creation.

BGE connection: The reversal of the modern virtue of pity. Directly opposes the self-contempt of 222 and the leveling of 219-221. Central to the book’s revaluation.

Modern companion insight: 2026 safetyism, trigger warnings, mental health industrial complex, and political promises of pain-free existence are the insane project of abolishing suffering. The free spirit cultivates the capacity to endure and transmute great suffering into creation, rank, and self-overcoming. Real excellence and culture are forged, not cushioned. Pity that prevents necessary hardness is the enemy of the future.
Validation: Direct Kaufmann-aligned quote on the discipline of suffering and pity against pity. ~340 words. Part Seven reversal of modern virtues preserved.
