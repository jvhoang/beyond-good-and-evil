FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886

Section 44

Identifier: 44 • Part Two: The Free Spirit (Closes the Part)
EXTENSIVE SUMMARY
Section 44 magnificently closes Part Two. The philosophers of the future will be free, very free spirits—but something more, higher, greater, and thoroughly different. Nietzsche feels an obligation to sweep away the stupid old prejudice and misunderstanding that has clouded "free spirit" like a fog: the modern, narrow, imprisoned, chained "free spirits"—levelers, slaves of democratic taste, without solitude, clumsy good fellows who want the universal green-pasture happiness of the herd, "equality of rights," and the abolition of suffering.

We opposite men think the enhancement of the species "man" has always required the opposite conditions: hardness, forcefulness, slavery, danger, stoicism, the art of experiment—everything evil, terrible, tyrannical, kin to beasts of prey and serpents serves the enhancement as much as its opposite.

The true free spirits are at home in many countries of the spirit, escaped from musty nooks, full of malice against the lures of dependence, grateful to need and sickness, curious to a vice, investigators to the point of cruelty, ready for every venture, jealous friends of their own profound solitude. "That is the type of man we are, we free spirits! And perhaps you have something of this, too, you that are coming? you new philosophers?"

Key quotes (Kaufmann):
"Need I still say expressly after all this that they, too, will be free, very free spirits, these philosophers of the future—though just as certainly they will not be merely free spirits but something more, higher, greater, and thoroughly different..." "We opposite men... think that this has happened every time under the opposite conditions..." "that is the type of man we are, we free spirits! And perhaps you have something of this, too, you that are coming? you new philosophers?"

Insight: The free spirit is the antipode of modern ideology and herd desiderata. Power, enhancement, and the revaluation require the "evil" and dangerous conditions as much as the good. Truth, morality, and the future belong to the rare, the profound, the attempters who embrace solitude and the full range of human drives. This crowns Part Two: from necessary falsification (Sec 24) to philosophers of the future who stand "beyond good and evil"—not liberal freethinkers but experimental, solitary commanders and attempters who refuse to be mistaken for the last men of democratic leveling.
Key Context: Climax and close of Part Two. Explicitly defines the free spirit against its modern abusers and points forward to the philosophers of the future who will carry the revaluation.
