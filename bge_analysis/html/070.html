FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886 • PART FOUR

Section 70

The Hand That Kills
Identifier: 70
MODERN INSIGHTFUL SUMMARY
One has been a bad spectator of life if one has not also seen the hand that in a considerate fashion—kills. The free spirit must witness and affirm the necessary, selective destruction that life requires for higher forms to emerge. Modern parallel: critiques of safetyism, meritocracy debates, and the courage to let outdated institutions or attachments perish.
