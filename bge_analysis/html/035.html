FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886

Section 35

Identifier: 35 • Part Two: The Free Spirit
EXTENSIVE SUMMARY
Section 35 delivers a stinging epigram aimed squarely at the moral corruption of inquiry: "O Voltaire! O humaneness! O nonsense!" Nietzsche mocks those who claim to seek truth only so they can "do good" with it ("il ne cherche le vrai que pour faire le bien"). The bet is lost before it is made—the "too human" motive guarantees one finds nothing worth the name of truth.

The free spirit refuses to subordinate the passion for truth to any utilitarian, humanitarian, or activist agenda. Truth is not a tool for improving the world or making people better; it is a dangerous, often uncomfortable discovery that the genuine inquirer pursues without moralistic guardrails. This is the same spirit that in the Preface and Section 1 unmasks philosophy's hidden moral prejudices.

Modern companion insight: In our time of "truth-seeking" that must serve social justice, public health messaging, equity outcomes, or "stopping misinformation," Nietzsche's warning is urgently relevant. When the search is pre-loaded with a desired moral result, it is no longer a search—it is propaganda or wishful thinking. The strongest minds insist on following the inquiry wherever it leads, even (especially) when the findings are inconvenient to "the good."
Key Context: Critique of moralistic truth-seeking (echoing Preface and Sec 1). Core to the free spirit's intellectual conscience.
