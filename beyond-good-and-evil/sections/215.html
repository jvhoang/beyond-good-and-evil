FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE • 1886 • PART SEVEN

Section 215

Motley-Coloured Actions: The Multiple Moralities of Modernity
Identifier: 215
EXTENSIVE SUMMARY (Faithful to Text)
Section 215 offers a striking astronomical metaphor for the moral condition of modern Europeans: “As in the stellar firmament there are sometimes two suns which determine the path of one planet, and in certain cases suns of different colours shine around a single planet, now with red light, now with green, and then simultaneously illumine and flood it with motley colours: so we modern men, owing to the complicated mechanism of our 'firmament,' are determined by DIFFERENT moralities; our actions shine alternately in different colours, and are seldom unequivocal—and there are often cases, also, in which our actions are MOTLEY-COLOURED.”

The “firmament” of modern life—with its competing inheritances from Christianity, Enlightenment, nationalism, science, and emerging democratic instincts—casts multiple, often conflicting lights on our choices. Actions rarely appear in a single pure hue; they flicker between moral codes.

Key Insight: Modern man is constitutionally hybrid. The free spirit does not pretend to a lost unity but learns to perceive the different moral suns and the motley illumination they produce, refusing self-deception about the equivocal nature of his deeds.

BGE connection: In Part Seven “Our Virtues,” this follows the recognition that our virtues are no longer the massive ones of our grandfathers. The multiplicity of moralities is both our limitation and the condition for new, more subtle experiments in value.

Modern companion insight: Contemporary individuals navigate overlapping moral ecosystems—professional codes, activist norms, family pieties, market incentives, algorithmic outrage—producing the same motley-coloured conduct. One can be compassionate on social media yet ruthless in career competition. The philosopher of the future cultivates conscious orchestration: to know which light one is standing under, to accept responsibility, and to will a new coherence.
